Yak-Recognition-and-Tracking-in-High-Altitudes-of-Arunachal-Pradesh(CV)

Prasenjit Kherkatary1, Sningrik D Sangma1, Laiphrakpam Indrason Meitei1, Rupesh Mandal2
1Department of Computer Science, Assam Don Bosco University, Guwahati, Assam, India
2Animal Physiology, ICAR-National Research Centre on Yak, Dirang, Arunachal Pradesh, India
Paper (Coming Soon) Code (Coming Soon) Dataset

Abstract

Automating the monitoring of individual yaks in remote, high-altitude shelters is critical for precision livestock farming, yet traditional physical tagging and manual headcounts are highly challenging to implement. In this work, we present YakFlow, an end-to-end computer vision framework that automates shelter occupancy tracking by detecting, tracking, and recognizing individual yaks from CCTV footage.

Framework Performance & Outputs

Figure 1: High-Precision Object Detection (YOLO11n)
Domain-adapted object detection results showing accurate bounding box predictions and high confidence scores across multiple yaks within the shelter environment.

Figure 2: Multi-Object Tracking & Identity Consistency (ByteTrack)
Real-time movement tracking visualizer indicating continuous track ID preservation and handling of transient occlusions under crowded gate conditions.

Figure 3: Deep Feature Re-Identification (ResNet50)
Extraction of deep feature embeddings from target crops and cosine-similarity matching against reference templates to ensure long-term identity maintenance.

Figure 4: Virtual Gate Crossings & Structured Analytics
Automated entry/exit event logging trigger zone and corresponding retrospective Excel logs reporting visit timestamps and shelter occupancy duration.
